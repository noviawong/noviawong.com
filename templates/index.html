Project Highlights


MealSpace was a team-based self-initiated project for 2018 CHI Student Design Competition. It is a solution aimed to solve the problem of college students lacking healthy eating habits. The process of user research and design iteration took place over the course of 4 months. My primary role was a UX researcher and we mainly utilized online surveys, paper surveys , as well as semi-structured interivews to collect user data.



Something Blue Consulting was a class project at the University of Michigan for the course on contextual inquiry. I worked on a team of 4 to assess existing problems of our client, develop interview protocols and conduct interviews with stakeholders, and analyze interview results using affinity wall. We found that their major problem was task distribution. This project spanned across 4 months and my primary role was interviewer and project manager.



Inspired by the flaws and constant frustrations of some of the modern account authentication systems, this study explored users' problems with password management and usage. The study was designed, conducted, and analyzed in five months. My role in this research was the primary investigator, in which I designed an online questionnaire and an implicit memory test to collect user data on password usage.
