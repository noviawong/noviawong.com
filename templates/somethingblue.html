Something Blue Consulting: Communications Between Organizations

Role: Researcher and Project Manager

Skills Used:
Contextual Inquiries
Affinity Walls
Literature Reviews
Public Speaking
Google Drive

Duration: 4 months

Summary:
Something Blue Consulting was a team-based course project. We were given three months to work on this project with a client. Our client approached us with a desire to update and increase their social media usage. After coducting six different contextual inquiries, my team and I organized the interview findings and our background research information, we put together an affinity wall. Through this analysis, we further identified additional issues that the organization was facing and brainstormed solutions around these newly identified issues. In the end, we suggested two solutions, one being the most practical and another being the ideal solution for the orgnaization. Given the nature of the organization, both solutions consisted of minimal technology usage, and instead focused on changing the way information was communicated between our client and another organization on campus.

Research Process

Process

Background Research

We began the project by conducting background research with our client and through literature review. Our intitial meeting allowed us to understand the problem scope from our client's perspectives. We learned about our client's expectations and the project goal of improving their social media and outreach strategy. We also learned that the organization itself and all staff members at the organization had minimal experiences with utilizing social media as a form of communication. It was also revealed during our initial meeting that some staff members were resistant to using social media in general. Specifically, we found that the organization mainly used email and website as the main forms of communication, with very small number of users on social media. To help us understand more about this specific topic, we each wrote a background research report on different aspects of the project, for example, I focused on researching the user type and characteristics on current social media platforms.


Outcome: Better understanding of problem scopes and served as the basis and fundamental of question development for contextual inquiry.


Contextual Inquiry

We conducted six interviews with stakeholders over the next five weeks. Interviews were conducted at interviewee's workplace in order for us to observe their work environment and their routine at work. Each interview was between 60 to 90 minutes, and was consisted of one primary interviewer and one notetaker. Interview questions were developed specific to each job role our interviewees held, with the exception of general questions. We decided to vary the key questions for each job role because we wanted to get a more thorough understanding of their perspectives and experiences with social media and outreach at the organization itself. Examples of general questions include:

  • Briefly describe your role at work.
  • How long have you been working in the organization?
  • When was the last time you use social media for personal reasons?

Interviews were recorded in audio forms with interviewees' permissions, and participant codes were assigned to each interviewee to keep confidentiality. These six contextual inquiries included observations of workplace, demonstration of tasks involving the system (in this case, various social media platforms), and semi-structured interviews with mostly open-ended questions.


Outcome: Specific instances and personal experiences of each interviewee with social media platforms. Observations were made regarding interviewees' work routine. For example, we found that one staff member still used flip-phone and was overloaded with administrative tasks along with communication on social media. This also allowed us to move forward to synthesize and compile the data for our next step.


Qualitative Data Analysis

After collecting interview data from our interviewees, we spent a total of 6 hours on putting together an affinity wall. We first went through a series of interpretation sessions of the six contextual inquiries, making notes of specific quotes and findings. We called these notes affinity notes, in preparation for the affintiy wall. We chose affinity wall for data analysis because it allowed us to string our findings together through as a form visualization. Putting together an affinity wall also allowed us to identiy specific issues that the organization was facing other than the problem that was originally identified by our client. We first wrote these affinity notes. In the end, we had a total of over 250 post-it notes and 4 main categories for findings.


Outcome: Four major findings about our client's intra-organization commuincation, improper workload- distribution, and lack of personal social media experiences.


Brainstorming Solution

The final phase of this project was to devlier suggestions and solutions in the form of written report and oral presentation. My team and I put together a 30 pages written report, detailing the most practical and the most ideal solutions we developed. We utilized a brainstorming matrix and assigned scores for each solutions for different factors we considered as important. These factors include budget, human resources, novelty, and technical skill level. The ideal solution we provided to our client was to hire a full-time professional commuincation lead, who would be reponsible to update social media on a regular basis. On the other hand, we suggested a more practical solution, which was to partner up with another organization, who had already offered assistance to improve our client's social media and outreach strategy.
