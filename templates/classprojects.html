Title: The Use of Virtual Machines in Learning About Humans Behaviors on Cyber Security

Abstract: Cyber security is often thought to be a topic in the computer science field. However, it involves more than just the codes and the framework; a secured network also requires users to be aware of the security situation and to take action. A common example is when the system alerts the users of firewall expiration through a popup notification. However, it is often difficult to know whether these alerts are influential in users’ decision-making regarding cyber security. One way to explore the extent they are influenced is to observe their behaviors when these alerts are presented to them. By using tools such as virtual machines, researchers can perform a study without harming the host system (the computer’s physical hard drive) while documenting users’ behaviors. These tools will also provide a virtual environment that stimulates a real computer setup. Virtual machines can also help researchers to understand more about how different factors (e.g., cost, time, cognitive capacity) affect users’ decisions in making cyber security choices. While this presentation focuses on how virtual machine technology can support research on human decision-making in cyber security, future studies can also utilize this technology to further improve the functionality of technology by incorporating human physical needs and their psychological needs.

Citation: Wong, N., & Schuster, D. (2016, April). The use of virtual machines in learning about human behavior in cyber security. Poster session presented at the Spartan Psychological Association Research Conference, San Jose, CA.





Title: Understanding Human Cognition in Computer Network Defense

Description: This is an ongoing NSF funded research study at San Jose State University, which I participated as a research assistant when I was an undergraduate student at the university. This study is led by Dr. David Schuster and his lab: Virtual Environments, Cognition, & Training Research Laboratory (VECTR). The current research focuses on developing solution for problems in the cyber security field through the perspective of human factors. The funded study particularly focuses on cyber network defense professionals and the relationship between human decision making models and their daily work.
